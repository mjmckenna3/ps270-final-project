Research Question

My research question is have US Court of Appeals judges become more politically polarized over time? My hypothesis is that judges’ ideological scores have, on average, become more polarized over time, given political parties have trended in that direction. Since presidents appoint circuit court judges, who then must be confirmed by the Senate, they will likely choose a candidate in line with their own political ideology. Therefore, political extremism will follow through in presidents’ actions. My explanatory variable would be time, measured in years, and it would vary by whether they were appointed by a Republican or Democrat. This is useful to see which political party appoints more ideologically extreme judges. The outcome variable of interest is political ideology, measured in the Jurist-Derived Judicial Ideology Score. An observed pattern in the data that would provide support would be the absolute values of the JuDJIS scores increasing as the year increases, a positive relationship. An observed pater that would disprove my hypothesis would be the average JuDJIS scores not changing over time, or the absolute values decreasing with time.

Trends are difficult to pick out in this graph. Rather, let’s look at the data as differences in the average Judjis ideology scores for each party over time. 
judges |>
  mutate(ideology = case_when(judjis_score > 0 ~ "Conservative",
                              judjis_score < 0 ~ "Liberal")) |>
  group_by(eval.year, ideology) |>
  summarize(mean_jscore = mean(judjis_score)) |>
  drop_na() |>
  pivot_wider(
    names_from = ideology,
    values_from = mean_jscore
  ) |>
  mutate(diff_in_means = Conservative - Liberal) |>
  ggplot(aes(x = eval.year, y = diff_in_means)) +
  geom_point() +
  labs(x = "Year", y = "Difference in average conservative and average liberal Judjis scores", title = "Changes in Political Polarization Over Time")

This model takes the average Judjis ideology score of all conservative judges and all liberal judges, which is determined by their score being positive for conservative or negative for liberal. The averages are arranged by year and subtracted to display the difference in averaage ideologies between conservative and liberal judges. The scatter plot displays a negative relationship between the year the difference in average ideologies between judges. Thus, it would appear judges have become, on average, less polarized since the year 1990.
